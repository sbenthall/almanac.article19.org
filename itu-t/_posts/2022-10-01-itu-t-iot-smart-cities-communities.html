Study Group 20 Internet of Things, smart cities and communities | ITU‑T SG20

This study group develops technical standards that delineate architectures, reference models, requirements, and capabilities for Internet of Things (IoT) and smart cities and communities systems and services. With more countries deploying infrastructure in the name of “smart” spaces, from shopping centres to workplaces, the extensive data being collected by sensor technologies normalises mass surveillance in these spaces, which can be used to monitor individual behaviour and movement.

  • Current work items: Requirements and Reference Architecture of Smart Education sets up the requirements, capabilities and architecture for smart education with the intention to improve and enhance learning and teaching efficiency in schools. The proposal includes methods for video monitoring and surveillance of the school environment and monitoring students’ behaviours and emotions. The highly invasive technology used for smart education presents issues of how this technology is deployed and the consent of the individuals, especially minors.
