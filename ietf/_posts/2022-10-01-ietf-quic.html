QUIC | quic

This working group focuses on the maintenance and evolution of the encrypted transport protocol QUIC, supporting its implementation and developing possible extensions to it. Created initially by Google and growing in usage, QUIC is designed to combine cryptographic protections with transport protocols, folding security and transport into the same mechanism. Despite this robustness, the deployment model for QUIC has long been that if it fails, systems that prefer to use QUIC will instead fall back to TLS-over-TCP. This means that a malicious network operator who objects to the metadata-hiding enabled by QUIC can simply block it and clients will return to the metadata leakage of the earlier default protocols. This can lead to eavesdropping into the information being sent, and ultimately determining which websites users are visiting.

  • Current drafts: Protected QUIC Initial Packets aims to develop ways to better encrypt the initial packets in a QUIC connection.
