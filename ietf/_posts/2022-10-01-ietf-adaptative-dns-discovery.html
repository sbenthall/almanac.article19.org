Adaptive DNS Discovery | add

This working group was responsible for defining the first IETF-standardized encrypted DNS transport (DNS over TLS). Its current focus on the link between DNS resolvers aims to limit the amount of data leakage that happens during multi-hop DNS resolution. It also defines the mechanisms through which a DNS resolver’s information is communicated, allowing the client to decide which DNS server to use. Sending DNS messages over encrypted transports to DNS resolvers can prevent malicious actors from snooping and reading the contents of DNS messages, and modify DNS traffic to block responses from selective queries. This can prevent internet service providers or government actors from monitoring users who access sensitive websites, or from blocking specific sites.

  • Current drafts: Discovery of Designated Resolvers aims to develop a mechanism for clients to discover the resolver’s encrypted DNS configuration in order to facilitate data packets to flow through encrypted DNS resolvers.
