Deterministic Networking | detnet

This working group develops routing and signalling protocols for deterministic networks under single administrative control. Deterministic networks are networks with features designed to provide explicit guarantees about message deliverability, hence increasing reliability and potentially guaranteeing low latency and zero data loss. With technologies and services such as video and audio streaming and real-time communications requiring real-time responses, deterministic networks aim to establish data flows that improve the quality of service and reduce any potential lag in the connection. Most feasible deterministic network deployments depend on single or closed-group administrative control over the network, so they are not relevant for Internet-spanning communications. Rather, this makes it possible for, for example, a large network operator to ensure that they meet a particular service level agreement for clients that use their network exclusively. There is an implication here that a client using such a network is willing to trust the network operator to provide these guarantees as these networks can potentially increase or reduce the risks to snooping and monitoring, and in turn the client's privacy and anonymity from being compromised.
