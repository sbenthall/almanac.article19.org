Technical standardisation is a critical stage in the design and development of internet infrastructure technologies before they are deployed in the real world. While many standards developing organisations aim to maintain open deliberation and consensus-driven decision-making processes, there are particular actors that are more dominant than others in this process. These actors define and influence which proposals and interventions are taken into consideration for standards development. The participation of these dominant actors in the development of technical standards significantly affects how civil society can effectively engage and contribute to decision-making processes within these organisations.

By understanding where industry actors dominate groups and committees, civil society can identify where these actors are more likely to standardise adversarial technologies and dedicate resources to push back against these technologies before they are deployed. Furthermore, civil society can identify potential actors, based on aligned interests, to work alongside and strategise with when intervening on proposals and drafts that have human rights implications.
