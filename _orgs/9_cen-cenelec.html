The European Committee for Standardisation | CEN and the European Committee for Electrotechnical Standardisation | CENELEC

CEN and CENELEC are two distinct standards organisations in Europe responsible for the development of standards and technical reports across various sectors. The two membership organisations collaborate on a broad range of technical standards in various fields, from optical fibre cables, energy supply, to IT service management. They can also contextualise ISO/IEC standards to be applied to the European context. Civil society can participate by paying and becoming a member of three designated member organisations that represent consumers, environmental and societal interests: ANEC, the European Trade Union Confederation (ETUC), and the Environmental Coalition on Standards (ECOS). This is one avenue for participation in addition to participating through national standards organisations. ANEC, ETUC and ECOS experts participate with equal rights in the working groupsâ€™ drafting of standards within the European Standards Organisations (ESOs). However, they have restricted participation as observers in technical committees, preventing them from making final decisions on standards and technical reports. Alternatively, civil society can participate through national standards organisations which have influence over these final decisions.
