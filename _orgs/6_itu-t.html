The International Telecommunications Union (ITU) is a UN specialised agency that is responsible for matters related to ICT infrastructure technologies. The ITUâ€™s Telecommunication Standardization Sector (ITU-T) in particular sets standards for ICT infrastructure technologies such as cellular networks. As ICT infrastructure technologies become increasingly ubiquitous, these standards determine how sensitive information is transmitted and stored in everyday communications. The ITU is a multilateral organisation where only nation states have voting rights. While civil society can technically become Sector Members, this may come at a large cost.
