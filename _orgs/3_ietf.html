Internet Engineering Task Force | IETF

The Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF) develops and promotes a wide range of internet protocols and standards at the logical layer, including the Internet Protocol suite (TCP/IP). Many of these protocols relate to the Domain Name System (DNS), the directory of the internet which translates domain names to IP addresses. This allows browsers and other tools to connect to the right website or other network service. These standards influence the way people design, use, and manage the internet. While IETF does not require formal membership and all participants are volunteers, a large number of participants are sent by their companies to create particular technologies, or add to their development. Draft documents and proposals at the IETF are known as Internet-Drafts (I-D). Once reviewed and published, they become a Request for Comment (RFC). RFCs can be classified as standard, informational, experimental, or historic.
